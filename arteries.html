Artery Conditions and Symptoms

Disorders with Chest Pain

  • Coronary Artery Disease (CAD): Chest pain (angina), shortness of breath, fatigue, heart attack symptoms.
  • Aortic Dissection: Sudden severe chest or upper back pain, shortness of breath, fainting, difficulty speaking.
  • Angina: Chest pain or discomfort, pain in arms, neck, jaw, shoulder, or back accompanying chest pain.

Disorders with Leg Pain

  • Peripheral Artery Disease (PAD): Leg pain or cramping, especially during exercise, numbness or weakness in the legs, coldness in the lower leg or foot, sores on the toes, feet, or legs that won’t heal.
  • Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT): Swelling in one or both legs, pain or tenderness in one leg, warm skin, red or discolored skin on the leg, visible veins.
  • Aneurysm: Often asymptomatic until rupture, but can cause leg pain or claudication if in peripheral arteries.

Disorders with High Blood Pressure

  • Atherosclerosis: Often asymptomatic until severe, high blood pressure, chest pain, shortness of breath, fatigue.
  • Renal Artery Stenosis: High blood pressure that is difficult to control, decreased kidney function, fluid retention, swelling in legs and ankles.
  • Coarctation of the Aorta: High blood pressure, headaches, muscle weakness, leg cramps or cold feet, nosebleeds.

Disorders with Sudden Severe Pain

  • Aortic Dissection: Sudden severe chest or upper back pain, shortness of breath, fainting, difficulty speaking.
  • Aneurysm Rupture: Sudden, severe pain in the abdomen or back, low blood pressure, rapid heart rate, fainting.
  • Embolism: Sudden severe pain, shortness of breath, chest pain, rapid heart rate, dizziness.

Disorders with Fatigue and Weakness

  • Coronary Artery Disease (CAD): Chest pain (angina), shortness of breath, fatigue, heart attack symptoms.
  • Peripheral Artery Disease (PAD): Leg pain or cramping, especially during exercise, numbness or weakness in the legs, coldness in the lower leg or foot, sores on the toes, feet, or legs that won’t heal.
  • Atherosclerosis: Often asymptomatic until severe, high blood pressure, chest pain, shortness of breath, fatigue.

Disorders with Shortness of Breath

  • Coronary Artery Disease (CAD): Chest pain (angina), shortness of breath, fatigue, heart attack symptoms.
  • Peripheral Artery Disease (PAD): Leg pain or cramping, especially during exercise, numbness or weakness in the legs, coldness in the lower leg or foot, sores on the toes, feet, or legs that won’t heal.
  • Embolism: Sudden severe pain, shortness of breath, chest pain, rapid heart rate, dizziness.
