Spleen Conditions and Symptoms

Disorders with Abdominal Pain

  • Splenomegaly (Enlarged Spleen): Upper left abdominal pain, fullness after eating a small amount, pain that may spread to the left shoulder, fatigue, frequent infections.
  • Ruptured Spleen: Severe left upper abdominal pain, tenderness when touching the upper abdomen, left shoulder pain, dizziness, confusion, lightheadedness.
  • Splenic Infarction: Sudden or gradual onset of left upper abdominal pain, fever, nausea, vomiting, shoulder pain.

Disorders with Fatigue

  • Splenomegaly (Enlarged Spleen): Upper left abdominal pain, fullness after eating a small amount, pain that may spread to the left shoulder, fatigue, frequent infections.
  • Mononucleosis: Fatigue, sore throat, swollen lymph nodes, fever, swollen spleen, headache, skin rash.
  • Anemia: Fatigue, weakness, pale or yellowish skin, irregular heartbeats, shortness of breath, dizziness, chest pain.

Disorders with Fever

  • Splenic Abscess: Fever, chills, left upper abdominal pain, tenderness, nausea, vomiting, loss of appetite.
  • Mononucleosis: Fever, sore throat, swollen lymph nodes, fatigue, swollen spleen, headache, skin rash.
  • Splenic Infarction: Fever, sudden or gradual onset of left upper abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, shoulder pain.

Disorders with Swelling and Fullness

  • Splenomegaly (Enlarged Spleen): Upper left abdominal pain, fullness after eating a small amount, pain that may spread to the left shoulder, fatigue, frequent infections.
  • Mononucleosis: Fatigue, sore throat, swollen lymph nodes, fever, swollen spleen, headache, skin rash.
  • Portal Hypertension: Swollen spleen, swollen veins in the esophagus or stomach (varices), abdominal swelling, confusion, reduced levels of platelets or white blood cells.

Disorders with Frequent Infections

  • Splenomegaly (Enlarged Spleen): Upper left abdominal pain, fullness after eating a small amount, pain that may spread to the left shoulder, fatigue, frequent infections.
  • Hypersplenism: Frequent infections, anemia, easy bleeding, fatigue, reduced number of blood cells.
  • HIV/AIDS: Frequent infections, fever, swollen lymph nodes, weight loss, night sweats, fatigue, sore throat.

Disorders with Anemia

  • Hypersplenism: Anemia, frequent infections, easy bleeding, fatigue, reduced number of blood cells.
  • Thalassemia: Fatigue, weakness, pale or yellowish skin, facial bone deformities, slow growth, abdominal swelling, dark urine.
  • Sickle Cell Disease: Anemia, episodes of pain, swelling of hands and feet, frequent infections, delayed growth, vision problems.
